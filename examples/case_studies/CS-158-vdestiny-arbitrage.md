## 1. The Core Mechanism

The "Escort Agency" model is often dismissed as illicit, but structurally it is a **pure "Logistics-as-a-Service" Marketplace**.

* **Operator (Platform)**: Provides Vetting, Scheduling, and Trust (Escrow).
* **Talent (Supply)**: Provides the Service.
* **Client (Demand)**: High-net-worth individuals seeking risk mitigation.

The Operator captures **~20-30% of Gross Transaction Value (GTV)** with **Zero Inventory Risk** and **Zero Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** (via Telegram/Word-of-Mouth).

## 2. Unit Economics (The "Risk Premium")

Data analysis of ~200 listings yielded a massive discovery in pricing psychology:

| Service Type | Avg Price | Insight |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Incall (Apartment)** | **$402** | The "Base Rate" for controlled environments. |
| **Outcall (Hotel)** | **$860** | **+113% Premium**. |

**The Lesson:** The market pays double not for the product, but for **Risk Transfer**. The price difference ($458) is purely a hedge against the variable volatility of an uncontrolled environment.

## 4. Supply Chain Tactics

* **The "Freshness" Hook**: Keywords like "Just Arrived" and "Fresh" appeared **245 times** in the dataset. The business manufactures FOMO by emphasizing the *contingent* nature of the supply (Visa expiry).
* **The Product Ladder**:
  * **Volume Tier ($270)**: Vietnamese supply (Entry Level).
  * **Premium Tier ($380)**: Chinese supply (Mid-Market).
  * **Luxury Tier ($500+)**: Russian/Brazilian supply.

* **Operator**: You (The Architect/Logistics).
