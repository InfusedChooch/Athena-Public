## 2. The Min-Max Analysis (Protocol 106)

### Solace Studios: Right of S (Diminishing Returns Zone)

```
U(c) curve is FLAT:
  - Each new outlet = +$500K CapEx (renovation)
  - Each new outlet = +$X/month OpEx (rent + staff)
  - Revenue grows linearly, but costs grow linearly too
  - Margin pressure increases as copycats enter
  - First store already closed (rental + competition)
```

**Diagnosis**: Operating in the **Orange Zone** (Luxury Trap). High CapEx, marginal incremental utility. Revenue might hit 7 digits ($1M+), but with 20 stores at $50K/year each, the profit margin is razor-thin or negative.

### Koocester: At the Sovereign Point

```
U(c) curve is STEEP:
  - Low CapEx (video equipment, one-time)
  - Low OpEx (no rent, no physical stores)
  - Revenue scales with clients, not real estate
  - High moat (deep domain knowledge + market position)
  - Already monopolizing Singapore, expanding to Malaysia
```

**Diagnosis**: Operating at the **Green Zone** (Sovereign). Maximum U/c ratio. Every dollar spent generates disproportionate returns because the cost base is nearly flat.
